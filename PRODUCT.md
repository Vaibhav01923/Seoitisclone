# Product

## Register

brand+product

## Users

Small business owners, indie hackers, and early-stage founders who want to know if AI engines recommend their brand — and who aren't deep SEO experts. They're time-constrained, results-focused, and typically managing their own marketing. Secondary: startup marketing teams running AI visibility campaigns across multiple brands.

Context: they arrive at the landing page curious ("does ChatGPT even know I exist?"), then live in the dashboard on a recurring basis to track progress after publishing content.

## Product Purpose

RankOnGeo tracks how AI engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok, AI Overviews) respond when asked questions relevant to a brand — and closes the loop by surfacing gaps, generating citation-ready content, and helping publish it. The pipeline is: measure → research → write → publish → re-measure. Success looks like a user seeing their brand appear in AI answers where it previously didn't.

## Brand Personality

Clever · Approachable · Modern

Voice: direct and confident without being intimidating. Uses plain language, not SEO jargon. Treats the user as smart but not necessarily technical. Has a point of view — doesn't hedge.

Tone: slightly energetic. Not corporate. Not hacker-dark. Like a well-designed tool made by people who care.

## Anti-references

- **AI slop in general**: the full pattern set — warm beige body (#f5ede3 and variants), gradient text, identical card grids with icon+heading+body repeated endlessly, numbered eyebrows (01 / 02 / 03) on every section, tiny uppercase tracked kickers above every heading, hero-metric template (big number + small label), glassmorphism as default decoration.
- **SEOitis / Peec AI aesthetic**: the direct visual ancestor we are moving away from — warm neutrals, histogram logo, SEO-tool clone feel.
- **Generic SaaS cream**: off-white bg, rounded cards, blue/purple accents — indistinguishable from 80% of AI-era B2B SaaS.
- **Aggressive dark-hacker**: terminal green, matrix vibes — would alienate non-technical users who are the primary audience.
- **Enterprise bloat**: navy + gold, dense data tables, BI-tool formality — too heavy for the founder/SMB user.

## Design Principles

1. **Pipeline over metrics**: Every surface should reinforce the measure → research → write → publish → re-measure loop. Don't show numbers in isolation; show what to do next.
2. **Approachable authority**: Feel expert enough to be trusted, simple enough that a non-technical founder isn't lost on first load. Never condescend, never overcomplicate.
3. **Earned distinctiveness**: Every design choice should pass the question "would an AI have defaulted to this?" If yes, find a different answer. Distinctiveness comes from choices, not decoration.
4. **Show, don't label**: Demonstrate AI visibility through the UI itself — real engine names, real response excerpts, real scores — rather than marketing copy asserting it.
5. **Speed as respect**: Fast load, instant feedback, no skeleton screens for content that can be preloaded. The user's time is the product's job to protect.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against background at all times — no muted gray on near-white. Reduced motion respected for all transitions and animations. Forms fully keyboard-navigable. Color is never the sole differentiator for status or data.
